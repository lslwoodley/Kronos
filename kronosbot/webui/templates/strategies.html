Kronos Strategy

How entries, exits, and sizing are decided.

NNFX Strategy Logic

1

Baseline (200 EMA)

Long when the close is above the 200-period EMA; short when below. This keeps the bot aligned with the long-term trend in both directions.

2

Primary Confirmation (ATR)

Require ATR(14) to be above its 20-period SMA, i.e. expanding volatility. This confirms the market is moving.

3

Secondary Confirmation (RSI 14)

Use RSI(14) in the 50-70 zone for longs and 30-50 zone for shorts. Avoid overbought (>70) and oversold (<30) extremes.

4

Kronos Forecast Bias

When a Kronos model is loaded, the next-day forecast return must agree with direction (bullish for long, bearish for short). Without a model this component is neutral.

5

Risk Management

Stop-loss is placed 2 × ATR(14) away from the entry price on both sides. Position size is fixed at 1 mini lot (10,000 units) for v0.
